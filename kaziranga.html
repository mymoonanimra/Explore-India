About Kaziranga National Park

Kaziranga National Park in Assam is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, globally famous for the one-horned rhinoceros. It is also home to tigers, elephants, swamp deer, wild water buffalo, and hundreds of bird species. Spread across the floodplains of the Brahmaputra River, it is a paradise for wildlife lovers and photographers.

How to Reach

By Air: Jorhat (97 km) and Guwahati (217 km) are the nearest airports.

By Train: Furkating Railway Station (75 km) is closest with good connectivity.

By Road: National Highway 37 passes through Kaziranga; regular buses and taxis available.

Top Attractions in Kaziranga

Jeep Safari: Explore central, western, and eastern ranges for wildlife spotting.

Elephant Safari: Early morning rides to spot rhinos up close.

Kohora Range: Most popular zone with easy access and rhino sightings.

Bagori Range: Famous for elephants, swamp deer, and migratory birds.

Brahmaputra Floodplains: Stunning views of wetlands and birdwatching.

Suggested 3-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival, evening Jeep Safari in Kohora Range.

Day 2: Morning Elephant Safari, visit Kaziranga National Orchid Park & Tea Gardens.

Day 3: Jeep Safari in Bagori Range, explore nearby villages, departure.

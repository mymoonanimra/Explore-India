About Rann of Kutch

The Rann of Kutch in Gujarat is one of the largest salt deserts in the world, famous for its surreal landscapes under the full moon and the vibrant Rann Utsav. It is a paradise for culture lovers, photographers, and stargazers, blending nature, heritage, and handicrafts.

How to Reach

By Air: Bhuj Airport (86 km) is the nearest airport with flights from Mumbai, Delhi, and Ahmedabad.

By Road: Good road connectivity from Bhuj, Ahmedabad, and Rajkot. State buses and private cabs available.

Top Attractions

White Rann: Expansive salt desert â€“ breathtaking under moonlight.

Rann Utsav: Cultural festival with folk music, dance, tents, and handicrafts.

Kalo Dungar (Black Hill): Highest point of Kutch with panoramic desert views.

Mandvi Beach: Serene beach with Vijay Vilas Palace nearby.

Handicraft Villages: Hodka, Nirona, and Dhordo known for embroidery, Rogan art, and craftwork.

Wild Ass Sanctuary (Little Rann): Home to Asiatic wild ass and migratory birds.

Suggested 3-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Arrive at Dhordo Tent City, enjoy Rann Utsav, evening at White Desert.

Day 2: Visit Kalo Dungar, handicraft villages, shopping at Rann Utsav.

Day 3: Mandvi Beach, Vijay Vilas Palace, departure.

Local Food & Culture

Food: Kutchi Dabeli, Bajra Rotla, Kadhi, Khichdi, Gujarati thali.

Culture: Rich folk dances (Garba, Dandiya, Raas), colorful handicrafts, camel rides.

Festivals: Rann Utsav, Makar Sankranti kite festival.

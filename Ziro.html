About Ziro Valley

Nestled in Arunachal Pradesh, Ziro Valley is a UNESCO World Heritage Site (tentative) and home to the Apatanis, one of the most colorful tribes of the Northeast. Known for its lush rice fields, pine-clad hills, bamboo groves, and cool climate, Ziro is a paradise for culture seekers, trekkers, and music lovers.

How to Reach

By Air: The nearest airport is at Tezpur (280 km) and Guwahati (450 km).

By Train: Nearest railway station is Naharlagun (100 km) near Itanagar.

By Road: Well connected by road from Itanagar, Guwahati, and other NE towns.

Note: Inner Line Permit (ILP) is required for Indian tourists and Protected Area Permit (PAP) for foreigners.

Top Attractions in Ziro Valley

Talley Valley Wildlife Sanctuary: Dense forests, orchids, and rare species.

Ziro Music Festival: Annual international music festival in September.

Apatanis Villages: Experience unique culture, tattoos, and traditional farming.

Meghna Cave Temple: Ancient 5000-year-old Shiva temple with great views.

Pine Hills & Bamboo Groves: Serene landscapes for walks and photography.

Suggested 3-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Explore Apatani villages, Meghna Cave Temple, and local markets.

Day 2: Trek to Talley Valley, enjoy pine forests and paddy fields.

Day 3: Attend Ziro Music Festival (if in September) or explore hilltop viewpoints.
